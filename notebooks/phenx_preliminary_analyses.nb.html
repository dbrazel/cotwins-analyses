In the initial in-person COTWINS assessments, twins were asked standardized questions about substance use, derived from the PhenX Toolkit. The goal of this notebook is to do some preliminary descriptive analyses and QC on these data.

Alcohol

Out of 670 twins, 254 admit to having at least one drink of alcohol. 143 admit to having had more than five drinks. The mean age of drinking initiation was 14.88, with this distribution, which shows some obvious outliers:

joined %>%
  ggplot() + 
  geom_histogram(aes(x = PHXQ0003, fill = Sex1), binwidth = 1) +
  labs(fill = 'Sex', x = 'Age of Drinking Intiation')

And the age at which participants began drinking once a month or more:

joined %>%
  filter(PHXQ0004 != 0) %>%
  ggplot() + 
  geom_histogram(aes(x = PHXQ0004, fill = Sex1), binwidth = 1) +
  labs(fill = 'Sex', x = 'Age of Regular Drinking')

We observe that the mean age of ever drinkers is 16.08 and that of never drinkers is 15.26. Summaries of other questions follow.

During the past 30 days, on how many days did you have one or more drinks?

joined %>%
  ggplot() + 
  geom_histogram(aes(x = PHXQ0005, fill = Sex1), binwidth = 1) + 
  labs(fill = 'Sex', x = 'Days of Drinking')

How does the number of days of drinking vary with age?

joined %>%
  ggplot(aes(x = Test_AgeYears, y = PHXQ0005, color = Sex1)) + 
  geom_jitter() +
  labs(color = 'Sex', x = 'Age in Years', y = 'Days of Drinking')

If we fit a linear model, we can see that age in years is not significantly correlated with days of drinking.

glance(lm(PHXQ0005 ~ Test_AgeYears, data = joined))

joined %>%

  labs(fill = 'Sex', x = 'Drinks per Session') +

How does the number of drinks per session vary with age?
